Impact of Poverty on lack of Healthcare, Smoker % and Obseity %
  • Poverty % varies from 9.2% from 21.5% across 51 states
  • The US average is at 14.9% with New Hampshire at the lower end and Missippi at higher end
  • A positive correlation exist between Povetry and 3 Y-axis factors (healthcare, smoker rate and Obesity % )
  • Using Pearson method we see that r2 values are 0.525, 0.520 and 0.462 for healthcare, smoker % and Obese% respectively
How does age correlate with availability of Healthcare, Smoker% and Obseity% ?
  • Population Median age across 51 states varies from 30.5 from 44.1 years
  • US average for median age is 38 years. Utah has lowest median age and Maine has highest median age
  • With respect to correlation, we find that age is negatively correlated to healthcare (-0.385 r2 value)
  • It has a weak positive correlation with smoker % (0.248) and no correlation either way with Obseity %
Income vs. Healthcare coverage, Smoker% and Obseity%
  • Population Median income across 51 states varies from $39680 to $73971
  • $54453 is the average income at US level. Missippi has lowest median income and Maryland has the highest
  • As income increase, lack of healthcare % drops. Thus showing negative correlation as confirmed r2 value of -0.434
  • Similarly, income is negatively correlated to Smoker % (-0.660) and Obesity % (-0.651)
